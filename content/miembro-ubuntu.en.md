## Want to join our community?

At Ubuntu Venezuela, the doors are open to everyone who wants to discover, use, or contribute to Ubuntu or Free Software. You don't need to be a programmer or engineer to be valuable; any type of collaboration strengthens us (translating manuals, answering questions, giving talks, or creating graphic designs).

### Steps to join locally (Venezuela)

1. **Register on Launchpad:** Our official platform for asynchronous work. Create your account at [Launchpad.net](https://launchpad.net) and join our group `~ubuntu-ve`.
2. **Introduce yourself:** Join our [official Telegram channel](https://t.me/ubuntuve) and participate. The best way to start is by greeting others and indicating what you'd like to learn or help with.
3. **Sign the Code of Conduct:** Understanding and respecting how we operate and treat each other by reading our official Code is an essential requirement to become an active collaborator (we'll soon enable the electronic signature form).
4. **Start Small, Lead Fast:** Begin by answering messages, suggesting improvements to our website, or helping secure a venue for our next FLISOL.

## Growing: Official Global Membership (Ubuntu Member)

The title of **Official Ubuntu Member** is not granted locally, but is a worldwide recognition given by Canonical's *Membership Board* to those who have made notable and consistent contributions (usually over a period of more than 6 months).

This is the rank held by leaders in our Member Profiles view. The benefits of taking this big step include:

#### Benefits:
- Right to vote worldwide in Community Council elections or surveys.
- A coveted `@ubuntu.com` email account and digital Ubuntu network alias.
- Real recognition that carries significant professional weight in CVs and portfolios.
- Free hosting in the community ecosystem for your open source technical blogs.
- Personal free subscription on corporate platforms like *Ubuntu Pro*.

#### What's the process?
To apply, you must build a presentation on the Ubuntu Wiki (*Your WikiPage*) attaching all your amazing contributions over the months. Then, you request a regional IRC/Matrix interview where those testimonials are evaluated.

**You don't have to do it alone!** The Ubuntu Venezuela board is fully available to sponsor your application once you've established a history of participation. Don't hesitate to reach out to us in the chat for ongoing feedback.
